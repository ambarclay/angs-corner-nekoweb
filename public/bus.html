the restoration timeline for my child

this whole adventure started in 2023 when i was trolling local craigslist and facebook marketplace posts for some kind of camper/ van/ school bus. originally i was looking for either an old rv that i could fix up and renovate, or a ram promaster/ ford transit type van what i could build out into a camper. i was kinda obsessed with the idea of van life at the time, and Joe was also into the idea of living in a van, working from home, and being able to travel wherever we wanted.

now, if you know anything about me, you know i've had a weird fixation on vw busses for a while. i couldnt tell you what started it... sometimes i think i was a total hippie in a past life (and i'm definitely a hippie in this life too).

well, one day my dad and i were looking for campers, and lo and behold we found a vw bus locally that was dirt cheap (for a vw bus anyway)- and it was my favorite color!!! and it had all of the parts and wasnt a complete rusted out piece of junk!!!! how was i supposed to say no? i went back and forth for a while about whether i should get it or not, but eventually the decision was made for me. the guy who posted it took down the listing after a couple weeks. my hippie dreams were officially dashed. i still searched for one occasionaly, but it was near impossible to find a vw bus in decent shape that was even close to what i could afford.

fast forward exactly one year, Easter 2024, and my dad and i were back at it again, scrolling the listings for campers. you'll never guess what we found. THE EXACT SAME VW BUS FROM ONE YEAR AGO! it must've been fate that reunited us. after briefly consulting with my dad and Joe, I immediatey messaged the guy to ask to go look at the bus. a couple days later we went to look at it, and i made the guy an offer on the spot. one week after that i went and picked up my baby.

given that the guy had all of the parts for the bus, and all in pretty good condition, my dad and i were thinking that it would be pretty easy to do the bit of work it needed and get it up and running. oh how wrong we were...
